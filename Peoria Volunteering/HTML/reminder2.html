Reduce, Reuse, Recycle

Reuse

If you can always try to reuse plastic goods. If you have a plastic bag already reuse it instead of throwing it out. Remember recycling doesn't solve every problem. Try to use anything you already have before getting anything new. It is always best to utilize preexisting goods.

Reduce

Not many people remember to reduce. Before anything else try to reduce the amount of plastic waste you use. Get a reusable water bottle, bring your own silverware places, don't use straws, etc... Remember its always better to not have any waste than to just recycle it.

Recycle

Recycling should be the last option. While it does help, not everything is recyclable. Before getting to this stage its important to reuse anything you can so that only a few things are recycled. And if you find garbage on the street recycle it.





Plastic Water Bottles



What is the effect?

  • It takes 1/4 of a bottle of oil to produce a single water bottle.
  • 90% of bottled water’s cost comes from making the bottle.
  • It takes 17 million barrels of oil to make one year’s supply of bottled water.
  • Bottling water releases 2.5 million tons of carbon dioxide into the atmosphere every year.
  • 38 billion water bottles (over 2 million tons) end up in U.S. landfills each year.
  • Plastic waste (including plastic bottles) washes into the ocean and kills 1.1 million marine creatures annually.

What can I do?

  • Buy a reusable water bottle
  • Reduce the mount of plastic you buy
  • Reuse the plastic water bottles you have
  • Recycle any bottles you haved left
  • Be conscience of what you buy
  • Clean up trash so it does not make its way into the ocean
  • Encourage others to follow your lead
